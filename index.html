Yinghao Sun

I'm an Assistant Research Fellow (2025 – ) at the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC) in Chengdu, where I work at the Computational Sensing and Intelligent Connected Vehicle Research Center under the guidance of Associate Research Fellow Shuguang Li and Prof. Tieshan Li. Previously, I was a senior computer vision algorithm engineer (2022 – 2025) at DJI Automotive (now ZhuoYu Technology), where I led the development of vehicle kinematics model-based multi-target tracking algorithms deployed on BYD Yangwang, SGWM Yunduo/Yunhai, and CHERY iCar models. I earned my PhD (2016 – 2022) from the Radar Technology Research Institute at Beijing Institute of Technology, under the supervision of Academician Mao Erke of the Chinese Academy of Engineering, where my research focused on radar signal processing and radar target detection and tracking. My doctoral thesis received the GNSS and LBS Association of China (GLAC) Excellent Doctoral Dissertation Award (2023) and the BIT Excellent Doctoral Dissertation Award (2022).

Research

I'm interested in Multi-Object Detection and Tracking, Multi-Sensor Fusion, and Radar Signal Processing. My research spans both automotive perception systems and radar signal processing, with a focus on practical deployment of perception algorithms in autonomous driving.
