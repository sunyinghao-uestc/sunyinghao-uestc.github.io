Yinghao Sun

I'm an assistant research fellow at the University of Electronic Science and Technology of China (UESTC) in Chengdu, where I work at the Computational Sensing and Intelligent Connected Vehicle Research Center under the guidance of Associate Research Fellow Shuguang Li. My research focuses on Multi-Object Detection and Tracking, Multi-Sensor Fusion, and Radar Signal Processing. Previously, I was a senior computer vision algorithm engineer at DJI Automotive (now ZhuoYu Technology), where I led the development of vehicle kinematics model-based multi-target tracking algorithms deployed on BYD Yangwang, Wuling Yunduo/Yunhai, and Chery iCar models. I earned my PhD from the Radar Technology Research Institute at Beijing Institute of Technology, under the supervision of Academician Mao Erke of the Chinese Academy of Engineering, where my research focused on radar signal processing and radar target detection and tracking. My doctoral thesis received the CSGPC Excellent Doctoral Dissertation Award (2023) and the BIT Excellent Doctoral Dissertation Award (2022). I serve as a reviewer for IEEE TSP, IEEE SPL, IET Radar, Sonar & Navigation, Electronics Letters, SCIENCE CHINA, and Physical Communication.
